# Corpus Analysis Method

## 1. Build a corpus map

For every supplied chapter record: title, arc/part, viewpoint, locations, scene types, opening method, closing method, emotional path and major imagery. Use this map to avoid conclusions based on one memorable passage.

## 2. Sample by function

Compare at least three examples when available for each relevant function: comic daily life, character entrance, briefing, investigation, mystery intrusion, relationship scene, action set piece, revelation, aftermath, prologue and epilogue.

## 3. Describe function, not surface tokens

Prefer “humor hides the viewpoint character's need and lowers reader defenses before an emotional reversal” over lists of slang or catchphrases. Prefer “sound bridges ordinary space into traumatic memory” over copying a distinctive metaphor.

## 4. Track counterexamples

For every proposed rule record where the corpus deliberately does something else. Turn absolute claims into conditional rules.

## 5. Propose evidence-backed rules

Each proposal must contain:

- observation;
- narrative function;
- source chapters;
- applicable scene types;
- counterexample or non-use case;
- confidence;
- failure mode if overused.

## 6. Require approval

Present proposed additions, revisions and retirements. Apply only the user's explicit structured choice. Preserve superseded rules in the style log or manifest history rather than silently deleting them.
